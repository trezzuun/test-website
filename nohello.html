Kindly avoid using just 'hello' in the chat

Picture dialing someone, greeting them with a hello, only to place them on hold... ü§¶‚Äç‚ôÄÔ∏è

Bob 2:15 PM

Bob 2:20 PM
what time was taht thing again?
Garxu 2:20 PM
oh - 3:30 mate

Note that Bob could have got his answer minutes sooner, and needn't have kept Garxu waiting. In fact, Garxu could have started thinking about the question right away!

People who do this are generally trying to be polite by not jumping right into the request, like one would in person or on the phone - and that's great! But it's 2023 and chat is neither of those things. For most people, typing is much slower than talking. So despite best intentions, you're actually just making the other person wait for you to phrase your question, which is lost productivity (and kinda annoying).

The same goes for:

  • "Hello, are you around?"
  • "hi sophia - quick question."
  • "You got a sec?"
  • "yt?"
  • "ping"
  • etc.

Just ask the question! üò´

If you find it too direct to just say 'Hi' and ask your question, feel free to add some pleasantries before your message.

For example:

  • "hey man, what's up? also, any idea when that thing's due?"
  • "Hi there! Hope you're well. I'm after the latest deck, when you get a sec :)"
  • "hey, if you're not busy, could you update those NFRs?"
  • etc.

Asking your question first enables asynchronous communication. If the other party is away, they can still respond even if you leave before they return, avoiding a 'Hello' without context.
